## English description

LunaPlate helps you understand your cycle and turn daily signals into gentle, practical care.

Record period dates, flow, symptoms, mood, pain, hydration, and private notes. See estimated cycle phases and prediction ranges based on your own history, then explore meal ideas, comfortable movement, and evidence-linked self-care suggestions that adapt to your current phase and check-in.

Features:

- Private on-device cycle history and daily check-ins
- Period predictions and irregular-cycle ranges
- Phase- and symptom-aware meal and movement ideas
- Evidence-linked care cards from recognized public health organizations
- Clear prompts for symptoms that may deserve medical attention
- Daily care checklist and local reminders
- Cycle and symptom trend charts
- Grocery list built directly from meal ingredients
- JSON backup and restore, including LunaPlate Web cycle data
- Built-in offline recommendations when the server is unavailable
- English, Simplified Chinese, and Russian interface

LunaPlate is not a medical device and does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any condition. Predictions and wellness suggestions are estimates and do not replace advice from a qualified healthcare professional.

## App Review notes

No account or login is required. All period history, check-ins, notes, settings, and grocery items are stored on device. The app sends only the current estimated phase and selected symptom identifiers over HTTPS POST to return meal and movement recommendations; the app automatically uses bundled offline content when the service is unavailable.

To review the main flow:

1. On Today, tap “Period started today”.
2. Tap “Check in” and save symptoms, flow, mood, pain, hydration, or notes.
3. Review the updated care cards, meal, and daily checklist.
4. Open Food, choose a meal, and add its ingredients to the grocery list.
5. Open Insights after adding at least three period starts to see a cycle-length trend.
6. Open Profile to test notifications, JSON export/import, privacy details, and delete-all controls.

The care content is educational. Red-flag notices explicitly direct users to healthcare rather than diagnosing. Source links open ACOG, the U.S. Office on Women's Health, or NHS pages.
